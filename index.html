About Me

I am a college English instructor turned full stack software engineer. I enjoy weaving my academic interests together with my new-found love of code, with a particular focus on the ways in which coding and technology can help make everyday life a little bit easier and a little bit better for everyone.

I began my journey to code in the fall of 2017. I fumbled around on websites like W3schools and MDN and spent a few bucks for online courses. These were ok, but they lacked the kind of deep and immersive educational experience that I had become accustomed to over the previous ten years of my academic life. It was enough, however, to get me thinking seriously about a career change. I spent a couple months researching bootcamps as well as more traditional (academic) routes, narrowing my choices down before finally deciding on Fullstack Academy of Code. I began my bootcamp career with the Remote Bootcamp Prep in a Month course followed by the 24 week Flex Part-Time Immersive in New York City. It was simultaneously the most rewarding, most challenging, and most frustrating endeavor I had undertaken in my life.

Skills

Code

At Fullstack, besides vanilla JavaScript, our technology focus was primarily on the NERDS stack:

  • Node
  • Express
  • React (with Redux)
  • Databases using SQL

In addition to these, we spent time learning some HTML and CSS. We also learned the fundamentals of Test Driven Development, primarily with Mocha and Chai. Additionally, we were encouraged to explore additional tools and technologies in our projects, such as Material-UI, Stripe, and Bootstrap (to name a few).

Creativity

As an academic in the humanities, and especially from an English Department with a rich history of philosophical discourse and post-structural attitudes, I consider my approach to any challenge or problem to be heavily reliant upon rigor and logic. However, within any formalized structure, it is creativity -- the ability to seek out new and meaningful connections within a framework -- that is of the greatest import.

I believe that this concept of finding meaningful connections translates directly to writing code; code operates within frameworks, but code often plays with rigidity and flexibility (for example, the notion of opionated vs. unopinionated software).

Compassion & Empathy

I strongly believe that compassion and empathy are much needed in all facets of the social spectrum. A compassionate and empathetic attitude is crucial in fostering healthier and more inclusive communities, however those communities are defined.

In order to build rapport with clients and coworkers alike, compassion is the key to understanding different perspectives while listening to and validating the needs of others. I believe in the importance of these attitudes in both social and professional interactions.
