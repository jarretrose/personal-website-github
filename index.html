About Me

For nearly ten years I had been pursuing a career as a college professor in English Literature and Composition. While I loved the work and my students, I was also enamored by the tech I could use to facilitate student learning, from Virtual Learning Environments and Whiteboards to Blogs-as-Essays and online-discussion forums.


I started thinking about the kinds of tools I would make, were I to have those skills, and thus my coding journey began (albeit slowly). As I started to pick my way through simple tutorials and documentation online, I realized that this was something I could be good at, something I would enjoy, and something that would allow me to do some good in the world (like teaching).


I committed to my coding journey in 2018, paring down my teaching responsibilities and enrolling in Fullstack Academy's Bootcamp Prep in a Month primer followed by their 32 week Flex Immersive Engineering Bootcamp. While the work was far more difficult than I had expected, it was also wonderfully satisfying. Trying to wrap my head around Objects for the first time was infuriating, but when it finally *clicked* -- that was like discovering a pattern in a novel for the first time. And just like in my academic life, these discoveries happen every time I sit down to code; I'm always learning, always seeing something a little bit clearer, always diving a little bit deeper, and always growing more curious.
